Description of the Mobility Match Tools


These occupational therapy tools are designed for occupational therapists, occupational therapy assistants, healthcare providers, parents, teachers, and individuals seeking to enhance an individual's mobility and independence. The three checklists support collaboration with an individual and their caregivers as well as healthcare and social service professionals to identify the least restrictive transportation options that align with the person's strengths, challenges, and available community resources. The resulting "match" report provides insights to develop targeted goals, helping individuals build the skills and abilities necessary for improving community mobility.


  • The Checklist for individuals with primarily intellectual disabilities was specifically designed to focus on questions about functional skills and abilities in areas of cognition, communication, and behavior related to using alternative means of transportation.

  • The Checklist for individuals with primarily physical disabilities was specifically designed to focus on questions about functional skills and abilities in areas of physical, cognition, communication, and self-monitoring related to using alternative means of transportation.

  • The Checklist for individuals with primarily autism was specifically designed to focus on questions about functional skills and abilities in areas of sensory, physical, cognition, communication, and behavior related to using alternative means of transportation.


For many individuals with developmental disabilities, there is no clear-cut diagnosis that fits neatly in each of these general areas. As more research is completed, we may find that one checklist may be appropriate, but currently, one checklist would be exceedingly long. It would be appropriate to use more than one with individuals that have more than one diagnosis to cover all the functional abilities and skill items.
