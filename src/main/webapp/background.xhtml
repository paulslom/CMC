Background


The initial concept of the three Mobility Match Checklists was identified in a response to a request for applications from the North Carolina on Developmental Disabilities (NCCDD. The call for the Community Living Mini Grants, September 2023, was to address NCCDD's Goal 2 in its Five-Year State Plan:

By 2026, increase community living for individuals with intellectual and other developmental disabilities.

Objective B, under Goal 2, specifically addressed transportation with a measurement of more individuals with I/DD will have transportation options to support community living and employment. Dr. Anne Dickerson submitted for one of the mini grants and was awarded the contract in December 2023.


In a previous project for the American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA), Anne Dickerson and Elin Schold Davis created an Occupational Therapy Checklist for Community Mobility (OTCCM) specifically for older adults who were no longer able to drive. Using this paper model, Dr. Dickerson proposed creating three checklists for three general categories of individuals: Individuals with primarily intellectual disabilities, individuals with primarily physical disabilities, and autistic individuals. Incorporating technology, she and her team designed each Checklists to "match" the various types of community mobility (other than driving) to the unique strengths and challenges of individuals with disabilities.


Ultimately, the Checklists were designed by Dr. Dickerson and her doctoral student, Ellee Hopkins, with expert feedback from Dr. Miriam Monahan. The Checklists were drafted and revised based on expert review by a group of occupational therapists who work directly with one or more the three populations.


Dr. Heather Panczykowski joined the team when Dr. Dickerson and Elle Hopkins applied to submit the Mobility Match for an I-Corps@ECU project to further its potential use in the marketplace. With funds awarded from I-Corps@ECU, the Mobility Match website was created and enhanced with additional monetary support from ECU's College of Allied Health Sciences' Office of Research.
